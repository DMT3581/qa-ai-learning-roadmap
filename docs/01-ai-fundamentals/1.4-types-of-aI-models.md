## In one line

AI models sort along two axes — *what they do* (generative = create new content; predictive = forecast or classify) and *what they work with* (text, image, code, audio, video, or several at once) — and knowing where a tool sits tells you what to expect from it.

## Why this matters for QA

"AI" covers very different kinds of model, and they behave — and get tested — differently. A generative model produces open-ended new content you have to verify; a predictive model produces a label or score you evaluate for accuracy. The modality tells you what goes in and comes out: can it read a screenshot, transcribe a call, generate an image? Placing a tool on these two axes is a fast way to set the right expectations — and it's the map underneath choosing a tool.

## Core content

### Axis 1 — what it does: generative vs. predictive

- **Generative AI** creates *new content* — text, images, code, audio, video — by learning patterns from data. It's the "creative" side: you prompt, and it produces something that didn't exist before. LLMs, image generators, coding assistants, and voice or music tools all live here.
- **Predictive AI** *forecasts or classifies* — it takes input and returns a label, a number, or a probability. It's the "analytical" side: fraud or not-fraud, churn risk, demand forecast, which items to recommend, is-this-a-cat. It analyses existing data rather than creating new.

A useful image: the two hemispheres of the brain — generative is the imaginative one, predictive is the logical one. They aren't rivals, and they often combine: predictive AI flags the customers likely to churn, and generative AI drafts the retention email.

### Axis 2 — what it works with: modality

- **Text** — LLMs, the tools you use most.
- **Code** — essentially specialized text generation (Copilot, coding assistants).
- **Image** — generation (make a picture from a prompt) *and* recognition (classify or describe an image — a predictive use).
- **Audio** — speech-to-text (transcription), text-to-speech, and music or voice generation.
- **Video** — generation and analysis.
- **Multimodal** — models that handle several at once (read an image and answer in text, take voice in and speak back). Today's frontier general models are increasingly multimodal.

### Put the axes together

Any AI tool is a cell in this grid. An LLM chat is *generative text*; Copilot is *generative code*; a flaky-test predictor is *predictive*; face unlock is *predictive image*; an image generator is *generative image*; a model that reads your screenshot and explains it is *multimodal*. Naming the cell tells you, at a glance, what to expect.

### Why it changes how you use — and test — them

- **Generative** output is open-ended and must be verified; there's no single right answer (see *Evaluating AI Outputs* and *Hallucinations*).
- **Predictive** output is a label or score you evaluate differently — accuracy, precision, recall, false positives and negatives.
- **Modality** tells you the inputs and outputs: don't expect a text-only model to read a screenshot, and do reach for a multimodal one when the task is visual.

## Examples

- **Generating test cases** → generative text; verify the output.
- **A model predicting which tests are likely to fail or be flaky** → predictive; evaluate its accuracy and watch false positives.
- **Analysing a screenshot of a failing UI** → needs a multimodal model (image + text).
- **"Is this AI feature generative or predictive?"** changes your whole test approach — open-ended validation versus accuracy metrics.

## Try it yourself *(optional)*

List the AI tools and features around you and tag each one: generative or predictive? which modality? You'll quickly see the grid is a fast expectation-setter — and that at least one "AI" thing you use is predictive, not a chatbot at all.

## Common pitfalls

- **Assuming all "AI" is generative.** A lot of valuable AI is predictive.
- **Testing a predictive model like a generative one** — or vice versa.
- **Expecting a text-only model to handle images or audio.**
- **Forgetting generative output always needs verification.**

## Key takeaways

- Generative AI creates new content; predictive AI forecasts or classifies.
- Modalities span text, code, image, audio, and video — and multimodal models combine them.
- Any tool is a cell in the two-axis grid; naming the cell sets expectations.
- Generative output is verified; predictive output is measured — test them differently.
